Projects: Escape Room
Course: Computer Science Principles
Duration: January 22, 2020 - January 30, 2020
Objective: To make an interactive game that includes multiple stages and increased difficulty and is about escaping a room.
Type of Project: Partner Project


To create this project, my partner and I coded with Python in a platform called Repl.it . We decided the objective of our game was to escape jail by solving three challenges. Each challenge varied in difficulty and depending on which challenge you chose, you may get a different ending to the game. For the first challenge, the user got to pick from a choice of three riddles to solve and each riddle had a different level of difficulty. The second challenge was also another riddle. For the third and final challenge the user was given a 6 by 6 map in which they had to find a shovel to dig there way out and the user was also limited to a certain number of tries to find the shovel. After finding the shovel the user had two options of escaping in which only one option, which was the most logical option, would lead to succesfully escaping and completing the game. My role in this project was to provide specific documentation for our code and to code the first challenge of our game. Throughout this project both my partner and I were able to succesfully comincate with each other and assist each other when needed to give our best product by the deadline. The biggest and significant challenge we had to overcome was planning too much for such a short worktime as we thought to accomplish too much and such little time given to work on our project. In this project I learned the importance of planning realistically and thoroughly before starting the project. This was also the first time in which I used python to create a game and I got a chance to learn the shortcuts and take future notes for myself while using python. In conclusion, my Partner and I used great teamwork and coding skills to produce one of the top Escape Room projects in our class. Projects: Santa Catcher
Course: Principles of Engineering
Duration: November 2, 2020- December 3, 2020
Objective: Design, test, and build a Rube Goldberg-style machine that can humanely capture Santa by lifting him at least two feet off of the ground when he visits a household.
Type of Project: Group Project


For this project my group members and I designed, modeled, and created a simulation of a rude goldberg machine that would succesfully and safely trap santa and raise him 2 feet in the air. As the project manager of this assignment I had to create and maintain the timeline and schedule for our project, while also dividing tasks for this project. To effectively achieve the goal of our project, I decided to split the project into 4 parts that we each had to deisgn and build. Personally, I was resposible for getting the Santa up a ramp to the required height of 2 feet. To accompliosh this goal I first decided to use and gather the following materials of 2 wheel and axles, 1 scissor, which classifies as a 1st class lever, a pendulum, and an inclined plane. My design begins when a ball from the part rolls and strikes a protruding spoke of a wheel and axle. This causes the wheel to turn and strike the protruding spoke of the adjacent wheel and axle causing both wheels to turn in opposite directions. The axle of these wheels is connected by string to opposite handles of a scissor that is perched at an elevation. The movement of the wheels causes the handles to compress causing the scissor to cut one string holding a hanging pendulum. This pendulum swings and strikes an object blocking the path of the skateboard containing Santa. Throughout the entire project, our entire group effectively communicated with another, conveying needs and modifications between each part and design so that our machine could work to perfection. Additionally throughout the course of this project, I learned how to incorporate ideas and techniques of simple machines into a real life problem and solution.
